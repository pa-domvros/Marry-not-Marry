Homework Reading: What Can We Learn from Arranged Marriages?

Have you ever seen TV shows about matchmaking and wondered about arranged marriages? While they might seem very different from what's common in Greece, this way of finding a spouse is more prevalent worldwide than you might think. Globally, 55% of all marriages are arranged. In a country like India, for instance, over 90% of marriages are set up by family members.

It is very important to understand the difference between an arranged marriage and a forced marriage. In a forced marriage, people have no choice, which is wrong because individual consent is a fundamental right. However, in most modern arranged marriages, both the man and the woman can say "no" to their parents' choice at any time.

Consider the story of a man named Daniel from India. His Christian parents looked for a wife for him who shared his deep religious faith and values. When they found a family with a daughter who seemed like a good match, they introduced the two. Daniel and the young woman were given a "courtship phase" to get to know each other. After several months of meeting, they both decided they were ready and got married. Today, ten years later, they are happy with two children.

This approach seems to be quite successful. The divorce rate for arranged marriages is only 6.3%, which is significantly lower than the 42% rate in America. This success might come from several key principles that can offer valuable lessons for any relationship.

Cultivating Love ‚ù§Ô∏è

In many Western relationships, the focus is on finding immediate passion and "chemistry." Arranged marriages teach a different approach. Couples often begin without knowing each other well, but they share the belief that love can grow over time. They focus on being patient and understanding, which helps them build a deep, loving connection as their relationship develops.

Commitment and Perseverance üí™

Couples in arranged marriages know from the very beginning that marriage requires dedication and hard work. They are committed to staying together and solving problems, rather than giving up when things get difficult. This strong sense of commitment helps them overcome challenges and build a long-lasting bond.

Building a Strong Foundation üèóÔ∏è

Arranged marriages prioritise building a solid foundation based on shared values, compatibility, and mutual respect. While intense romantic feelings are exciting, they can sometimes fade. By first making sure a couple is compatible in the most important areas of life, they create a stable partnership that is truly built to last.

Weathering the Storms ‚õàÔ∏è

Every relationship will face difficult times and misunderstandings. Arranged marriages show the importance of facing these challenges together. Instead of giving up, these couples rely on good communication, compromise, and their shared commitment to find solutions. This helps them get through the hard times and can even make their relationship stronger.

Growing Together üå±

In this view, love isn't just a destination; it's a journey. Arranged marriages encourage couples to adapt, compromise, and evolve together throughout their lives. People change over time, and for a relationship to succeed, it must be able to change too. This mindset of continuous growth keeps the partnership dynamic and strong.

Even if this isn't the path for you, we can all learn from the principles that make these marriages so successful. The dedication to hard work and the blessing of having family support are lessons that can help any relationship succeed.
